What is this?

Flocking is a behavior exhibited by birds in groups in flight. This simulation shows the behavior of flocking, and allows you to tune parameters to see different behavior. The three main factors in flocking are cohesion, alignment and separation . The combination of these three factors forms the pattern we know as flocking.

Cohesion is the force that pulls a bird towards the average center position of all neighbors within a radius (Neighbor Radius).

Alignment is the force that steers a bird in the same direction as the average of the neighbors.

Separation is the force which keeps birds from flying too close to each other, keeping some space in between them.
